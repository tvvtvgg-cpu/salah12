Why I Started This Website (And Why It Took So Long)

salah12 · April 6, 2026 · 4 min read

I've had this idea for years.

A place to write. A place to document. A place to say things out loud that I'd been keeping inside my head or my notes app or nowhere at all.

Every time I almost built it, I'd stop. Not because I didn't have anything to say — I had too much. I stopped because I wasn't sure it was good enough. Polished enough. Ready.

Here's what I eventually realized: that feeling never goes away. You don't wake up one day feeling ready to be seen. You just decide to show up anyway.

So that's what this is. It's not a finished product. It's a running document of someone who is in the middle of it — building, thinking, failing quietly, occasionally getting something right.

Why it took so long

Partly fear. Partly perfectionism. Partly not knowing what I'd say or who I was saying it to. The usual reasons people don't start things.

But mostly I think I was waiting for some version of myself that was worth documenting. A more figured-out version. A version that had something useful to teach.

What I eventually understood is that the process is the thing. Not the destination. Not the finished, polished result. The actual messy work of trying to get somewhere — that's what's worth writing down.

What this site is

It's not a portfolio. It's not a brand. It's not a content strategy.

It's just a place where I write things down. Stories, ideas, things I'm working on, things I'm thinking about. Sometimes useful, sometimes not. Always honest.

I don't know who will read this. Maybe no one. Maybe someone who needed to see that they're not the only one figuring it out as they go.

Either way, I'm writing it.
